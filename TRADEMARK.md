# PlayTranslate Trademark Policy

The PlayTranslate **source code** is licensed under the GNU General Public License v3.0 (see `LICENSE`). This policy covers something the GPL does *not* cover: the **PlayTranslate name, logo, and visual identity**.

The goal is simple: keep the code open and forkable, while preventing confusion about what is and isn't the official PlayTranslate app.

## What this policy covers

The following are **trademarks of the PlayTranslate project** (referred to here as "the Marks"):

- The name **"PlayTranslate"** (and stylized variants such as "Play Translate")
- The **PlayTranslate launcher icon** and any other official logos, wordmarks, or app-store graphics
- Any other branding the project uses to identify itself (splash screens, marketing imagery, store listings)

These Marks are **not licensed under the GPL**. The GPL grants you broad rights over the code; it does not grant you rights over the brand. This is the same approach used by Mozilla (Firefox), the Linux Foundation, and most major open-source projects.

## What you can do without permission

You can freely:

- **Use the code.** Fork it, modify it, redistribute it, sell it, or embed it in your own projects under the terms of the GPLv3.
- **Refer to PlayTranslate by name** in articles, reviews, tutorials, blog posts, videos, academic papers, bug reports, and other discussion. Nominative use ("works with PlayTranslate", "imported from PlayTranslate", "compared to PlayTranslate") is fine.
- **State that your project is a fork of PlayTranslate** or "based on PlayTranslate," provided it's clear your project is not the official one.
- **Use the icon and screenshots** in reviews, tutorials, and editorial coverage.

## What requires a different name and icon

If you **distribute a modified version of PlayTranslate** — including but not limited to publishing it on Google Play, F-Droid, the Amazon Appstore, a website, or any other channel — you must:

1. **Change the app name.** Do not call your fork "PlayTranslate," "PlayTranslate Pro," "PlayTranslate Plus," "PlayTranslate (modified)," or any other name that incorporates "PlayTranslate" or a confusingly similar variant.
2. **Change the launcher icon and any other branding.** Your fork must be visually distinguishable so users do not confuse it with the official app.
3. **Change the application ID** (currently `com.playtranslate`) to something under your own namespace.
4. **Not imply endorsement.** Do not describe your fork as "official," "authorized," "endorsed by," or "affiliated with" the PlayTranslate project unless you have written permission.

You may, and are encouraged to, state factually that your fork is **"based on PlayTranslate"** or **"a fork of PlayTranslate"** in your description and documentation.

## What is never permitted without written permission

- Publishing an app to any app store using the PlayTranslate name or icon, except by the project maintainers
- Using the Marks in a way that suggests official status, endorsement, or affiliation
- Registering domain names, social media handles, trademarks, or company names that incorporate "PlayTranslate" or confusingly similar variants
- Using the Marks in advertising for unrelated products or services

## Why this policy exists

Open-source licenses protect the freedom to use and modify code. Trademark policies protect users from being misled about the origin of the software they install. Both matter:

- Without the GPL, the code wouldn't be free.
- Without this policy, anyone could publish a modified — possibly malicious — app to the Play Store under the PlayTranslate name and icon, and users would have no way to tell it apart from the real one.

This policy is intentionally permissive about discussion, education, and forking. It is intentionally strict about distribution under the PlayTranslate brand.

## Requesting permission

If you'd like to use the Marks in a way this policy doesn't permit — for example, a community translation, a hardware bundle, an integration that wants to use the official branding — please open an issue on the project's GitHub repository or reach out via the [Discord server](https://discord.gg/DVCj6p7MUC).

## Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated as the project grows. Material changes will be announced via the project's release notes.
